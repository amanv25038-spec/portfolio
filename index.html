About Me

MSc Physics student at IIT Mandi, India

I'm a postgraduate student at the Indian Institute of Technology Mandi, pursuing an MSc in Physics. My academic interests revolve around quantum mechanics, atomic physics, and the beautiful mathematics that underlies the physical world.

I enjoy exploring problems computationally — writing simulations, implementing numerical methods, and visualising physical phenomena in code. I've worked with C and Python, and have a particular fondness for clean, well-documented programs.

Outside physics, I read broadly — history of science, mathematics, and occasionally philosophy. I believe the best scientists are also good writers, and I try to improve both skills constantly.

I'm actively looking for research internships and collaborations in theoretical or computational physics. If you're working on something interesting, I'd love to hear from you.
